Limitless

2025
TBD TBD

Limitless is one of a few Data-Driven Performance works that have been written, processed, and will be assimilated into my portfolio based on my experience with live signal processing and audio manipulation in real time with data-driven controllers. This piece is in its conceptual phase that utilizes live continuous data streams and multiple layers of software in order to create a multi-sensory experience for the audience. The inspiration for this concept comes from my earlier work with a singular accelerometer-based controller that manipulates audio and visuals in real time.

Three composers/performers who have heavily influenced this performance concept are Eli Fieldsteel, Pamela Z, and Imogen Heap who have pieces and performances derived from custom made glove controllers and live electronics. Through various means of data mapping, data transfer to multiple layers of software, and real-time audio/visual manipulation, the byproduct of these various pieces and performances culminate into a multi-sensory experience that can be perceived by the audience and performer as an organic engagement between human and machine. The feasibility and ease of performativity with something as intimate as a wearable glove controller acts as an impetus for my creative process regarding live performance and engagement with non-traditional interfaces.

If We Could Forget It Gently Together

TBD
TBD TBD

If We Could Forget It Gently Together is the second work in the Vestige series, continuing an inquiry into how memory forms, reshapes, and dissolves—especially those memories that exist more in longing than in lived experience. Where Vestige asked what remains after touch, this piece reflects on the moments that never happened—the ones suspended in potential, rewritten in imagination, or gently fading before they ever fully formed.

This work is both performance and allegory, placing the performer and audience within a shared emotional architecture. It explores how absence can speak, how silence becomes a vessel for memory, and how we sometimes remember what never was. It is about mutual misremembrance, the intimacy of imagined histories, and the quiet ache of untold connection.

Technically, the piece is built on a distributed performance system that spans both a laptop and a desktop, networked to communicate in real time. A custom 3D-printed object embedded with an Arduino-driven gyroscope and accelerometer captures the performer's motion. This data is routed through Processing and Max/MSP, then sent across the local network to manipulate parameters in Ableton Live running on both machines.

The sound structure is built around dynamic interaction between live and prerecorded elements. A Moog Matriarch provides real-time analog synthesis, processed through a Chase Bliss Mood pedal and mixed via a Mackie 1202-VLZ3. These live signals are blended with cassette playback from a Yamaha MT50 four-track, carrying fragmented pre-recorded textures and voices. The resulting audio is routed through VoiceMeeter for simultaneous monitoring, control, and live streaming.

The system responds fluidly to motion—parameters such as delay, filter cutoff, pitch modulation, and spatial position shift in real time based on the orientation of the handheld object. But when the performer is still, the system resists resolution. Sound holds its breath. In this way, absence becomes an active compositional force, reflecting the piece's deeper emotional concerns.

If We Could Forget It Gently Together exists in the in-between—between touch and silence, memory and imagination, audience and performer. It is about the stories we almost lived, the words we never said, and how forgetting—if shared—can still hold something tender.

My Festering Synapses

TBD
Fixed Media TBD

During my first semester of compositional studies with Dr. Elainie Lillios my compositional and creative efforts have been hyper focused on recreating the sonic world that is fixed media. I was bombarded by timeless classics of the electroacoustic canon such as François Bayle, Luc Ferrari, Hildegard Westerkamp, Trevor Wishart, Bernard Parmegiani, Denis Smalley, and many others. For this piece the main influence came from Denis Smalley and his piece “Pentes” written for fixed media in 1974. The characteristics of each sound object, followed by abrupt and explosive gestures seemed to captivate me and mold my philosophy in which electroacoustic music should behave and develop over time.

“My Festering Synapses” attempts to achieve a behavior similar to Smalley’s “Pentes” but carries the weight of everything else that has been guiding me throughout that life changing semester. Every moment that was dedicated to the discovery of new sounds and compositions, new styles and methods of composition, and new catalogues of information that accompanied these pieces, my mind was left to stay in this state of constant mental stimulation. These thoughts began to fester into my own psyche, continually pushing myself to compose and create in this cyclical pattern of intrigue, discovery, reflection, and creation. All the sounds present in this piece have been sourced from an acoustic sound source and then manipulated digitally. Some of these sounds have been produced from my voice alone but others have been sonically snapshotted from my immediate surroundings as I composed this piece.

Premiered as a 4-channel fixed media piece at the Internationell Elektroakustisk Festival · Slottsbiografen Theatre, Uppsala, Sweden · Performed at MOXsonic Festival · 2026 · Warrensburg, MO

Sonic Apparitions

2022
TBD TBD

“Sonic Apparitions” was my first premiere piece utilizing fixed media and acoustic instruments. The premise revolves around the performer playing unconventionally and responding to a “ghostly apparition” represented by the fixed media track. Both elements of the fixed media ghostly persona and the rough and crass nature of non-traditional performance techniques create a sudo dialogue between what is sonically tangible and what is beyond our grasp and our own reality.

Special thanks to violinist Sadie Hamrin for helping me realize this piece and being such a great performer to work with regarding newly introduced extended techniques.

Performed at IMFA 2022 · Duino, Italy

About

Julian Green is an electroacoustic composer and performer currently based in Eugene, Oregon.

Green’s work centers on data-driven instruments and live electronics, exploring the intersection of physical gesture, custom hardware, and sonic transformation. Key works include Again for Gametrak controller and Kyma, If We Could Forget It Gently Together: Vestige Series for custom 3D-printed gyro controller, and Breakthroughs for Wacom Tablet. His fixed media piece The Inconsistent Continuities was commissioned by the Kingler Electroacoustic Residency at Bowling Green State University and professionally recorded by the Hypercube Ensemble.

Green’s music has been presented internationally, including at the Internationell Elektroakustisk Festival at the Slottsbiografen Theatre in Uppsala, Sweden, the International Music Festival in Duino, Italy, and most recently at the MOXsonic Festival in Warrensburg, MO, where his fixed media piece My Festering Synapses was featured. He is the recipient of the Kingler Electroacoustic Residency at Bowling Green State University and a podcast internship with the Global Hearth Podcast at the University of Oregon.

Upcoming performances of Again are scheduled for the International Computer Music Conference (ICMC) in Hamburg, the SEAMUS National Conference in San Antonio, and the New York City Electroacoustic Music Festival (NYCEMF). Green holds a B.M. in Music Composition from Arkansas State University and an M.M. from Bowling Green State University, and is currently a PhD candidate in Music Composition at the University of Oregon.
